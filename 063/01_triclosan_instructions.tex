\subsection*{Instructions for making Triclosan solutions}

Each semester needs 1 \si{g} Triclosan. Order from Sigma Aldrich, catalog \textsc{phr}1338-1\textsc{g}.

Students work in groups of 3â€“4 (one table). Each table gets five small jars, one for each solution.

You need five stock solutions total, all in 50\% ethanol, including control. The five concentrations are 0.0\% (control), 0.1\%, 0.3\%, 0.5\%, and 0.7\%.

\begin{enumerate}
	\item Dissolve 0.7 g in 100 mL of 50\% ethanol. This is the 0.7\% stock solution.
	
	\item Remove 42 mL of the 0.7\% solution and place it in clean jar labeled 0.5\%. 
	
	\item Add 17 mL of 50\% ethanol to this jar. This makes 59 mL of is the 0.5\% stock solution.
	
	\item Dissolve 0.3 g in 100 mL of 50\% ethanol. This is the 0.3\% stock solution.
	
	\item Remove 20 mL of the 0.3\% solution and place it in a clean jar labeled 0.1\%.
	
	\item Add 40 mL of 50\% ethanol to this jar. This makes 60 mL of the 0.1\% stock solution.
	
\end{enumerate}

Aliquot approximately 9 mL of each solution into the appropriately labeled small jars for use in lab.

Place one paper dot for \emph{each} lab section into each of the filled small jars. For fall with 9 sections (typically), add nine paper dots. For spring with four sections (typically), add four dots.
